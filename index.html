It's... a... musical!

In the past few years, I've taken on an interest in Broadway and musical theater. In 2016 my wife and I visited New York and saw a few shows between stops at all the usual tourist attractions.

  • Hamilton
  • Waitress
  • Something Rotten
  • Shuffle Along...

Hamilton

Date seen: March 25, 2016
Theater: Richard Rogers Theatre

Hamilton was (and still is, as of this writing) the hottest Broadway sensation of 2016. Tickets are next to impossible to get, and ridiculously expensive. Daily, however, there is a lottery that fans can sign up for to get front row seats for $10 each. As luck would have it, we won, and saw what has become - for me - my absolute favorite musical.

We had to cancel our tickets to see The King & I, but it was very much worth it, to get front-row tickets to the most popular show on Broadway for only $20!

Waitress

Date seen: March 26, 2016
Theater: Brooks Atkinson Theatre

Music and Lyrics by Sara Bareilles, and based on the movie by the same name. The movie starred Kerri Russel, but the musical starred Jessie Mueller, who previously won a Tony for playing Carol King in Beautiful.

Something Rotten

Date seen: March 27, 2016
Theater: St. James Theatre

Something Rotten is a comedy musical starring Brian D'Arcy James and Christian Borle. It's a hilarious comedy about the (fictional) creation of the first ever musical, that pokes fun at all the musical tropes we all know and love - dance breaks, randomly breaking into song, and the strangely acceptable all-sung shows like Les Miserables.

Shuffle Along, or, the Making of the Musical Sensation of 1921 and All That Followed

Date seen: March 26, 2016
Theater: Music Box Theatre

Normally, one would jump at the opportunity to see Audra McDonald live on Broadway. We were no exception to this rule. Problem being: while Audra was great, this show was horrrrible. Shortly after we saw it, it closed. I don't think anyone is particularly bothered.
